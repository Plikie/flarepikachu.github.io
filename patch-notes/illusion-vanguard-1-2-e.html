Illusion Vanguard Update - v1.2 Title Change Explanations

Overview

This title update revolves around three things: The introduction of activated abilities, nerfing all energy-related stats on weapon titles, and buffing the impact of damage % on weapons.

Activated abilities allows players to better control when they want their weapon ability to activate. This should be a welcome change, other than the fact that you'd have to get used to pressing your 9 slot/key to activate this.

The decision to nerf energy gain from Legendary Weapons was twofold: Firstly, high base values of energy gain can be easily boosted by Weapon Upgrades and Star Pieces towards huge values. Secondly, having such high energy generation for weapon titles could lead to balancing issues with future content.

As such, we have decided to drastically reduce the base values of Energy per Second/Energy per Hit that weapons provide, with the highest amount being 4 to stop the base value from being scaled too high.

For Vigorous and Suspicious, we elected to move most of their energy regeneration to their ability, meaning that the bulk of these titles' energy generation is not boosted by Weapon Upgrades nor Star Pieces. Suspicious received a blanket nerf but should still function similarly as before, albeit with much more RNG and cost to set up.

While overall legendary energy gain was reduced, we drastically buffed titles that offered damage bonuses. We previously overestimated the impact of a 15% damage boost, which when put into perspective of a system where skill tree upgrades give upwards of 80% Damage/Healing bonuses, that 15% damage boost didn't matter all that much. Allowing Divine and Fervent to stack up to 60% Damage Bonus means that skilled players can benefit from maintaining the damage boost's uptime as long as they play in a deliberate way.

Requiem was intended to be great but not exclusive for Revenant and now is equipped to directly benefit the Revenant class greatly. You can easily maintain your 5 stacks of Requiem by constantly activating Undying Army, but at the cost of... playing Revenant? :). Whether or not to utilize this weapon title is your decision to make!

Stalwart now has an additional ability that prevents you from being oneshot from one damage instance as long as your HP is above 80%. This covers a previous weakness of the title being unable to cover for when your HP is above 80% and you take a ton of damage. There isn't much use-case other than Narmer currently, but this can change very soon.

Enhanced now has an additional ability that boost the duration of the active abilities of allies, such as Inferno, Berserk, etc. This duration increase is triggered for each ally "hit" by the caster's blue rune (slot 4).

The list of abilities that can trigger this boost includes:

  • Paladin - Holy Radiance,
  • Defender - Intervene,
  • Earthwarden - Chain Heal,
  • Vindicator - Bubble,
  • and Apothecary - Remedic Chains.

I hope you enjoy figuring out how these abilities interact with the Enhanced title, as some of these abilities don't interact as simply as it seems.

Stacking Mechanic

  • TL;DR: Certain weapon abilities now have effects that stack additively with one another.
  • There's nothing in particular with this change, I just thought designing title abilities with a stacking system opens up design space for buffing individual titles by increasing the amount of times their weapon ability can stack, and opens up some cool interactions with enemies...

Activated Abilities

  • You can now choose when to activate your weapon's ability! This allows you to better control when you want the ability effect to trigger, letting you use your weapon abilities to their maximum potential.

Weapon Balance Changes

Divine
  • EPS: 7 -> 3
  • EPH: -13 -> -7
  • Rework the concept of Divine to be similar to Requiem/Fervent with a stacking system:
    • The effects of Divine stacks are additive to one another.
    • Divine can have a maximum of 3 stacks.
    • Gain a 20% Damage Boost for 30 seconds when you deal damage 40 times.

Enhanced
  • Enhanced now also adds active ability duration to all allies targetted by the title-holder's blue rune abilities (Slot 4).

Fervent
  • Rework the concept of Fervent to be similar to Requiem with a stacking system:
    • The effects of Fervent stacks are additive to one another.
    • Fervent can have a maximum of 3 stacks.
    • Gain a 20% Damage Boost for 30 seconds when you lose 5,000 health (take 5000 damage post damage reduction).

Requiem
  • Reworked the concept of Requiem interacting with Undying Army:
    • The effects of Requiem stacks are additive to one another.
    • Requiem can now have up to 5 stacks.
    • Requiem still lasts 60 seconds.
  • A stack of Requiem can be obtained:
    • When a teammate dies,
    • When a teammate has Undying Army applied to them, and
    • When a teammate pops under Undying Army.

Suspicious
  • Energy per Hit: 20 -> 3
  • Passive ability has been modified to:
    • Play an amogus sound and gain 20 energy whenever you land a melee crit.

Stalwart
  • Added an additional passive ability to Stalwart:
    • If the player's HP is currently higher than 80% and the player will die from the next source of damage (e.g Narmer BOOM), set the player's health to 5% of their max HP and gain 99% damage reduction for 5 seconds. (Cooldown: 30s)

Vigorous
  • Energy per Second: 4 -> 2
  • Passive ability is now an activated ability with the following effect:
    • +20 Energy per second for 10 seconds after using 400 energy.

Vorpal
  • Energy per Hit: 7 -> 4
  • Melee Damage: 180 - 200 -> 200 - 220
